🍎 THE SUPER BUDGET CIDER GUIDE — VOLUME BREWING EDITION 🍺

✅ GOAL

Make ~12% ABV cider in reused water bottles using only cheap, supermarket-available ingredients. Scalable. Accessible. Tastes like premium cider (think Kopparberg, Rekorderlig, etc.) on a student budget.

🧪 RATIOS FOR ~12% ABV

  • Sugar: ~90g per litre (180g per 2L)
  • Yeast: Just a pinch (around 1g)
  • Balloon: 1 per bottle, with 1–2 holes poked using a needle

🔧 BREWING INSTRUCTIONS (Per 2L Bottle)

  1. Drink/empty and rinse water bottle.
  2. Funnel in:
    • 2L summer fruits juice drink
    • 180g sugar
    • A small pinch (1g) of yeast
    • (Optional but recommended) 1 tea bag, ½ tsp lemon juice, a pinch of cinnamon
  3. Fit balloon over bottle opening and poke 1–2 small holes.
  4. Leave at room temperature (18–22°C) for 7–10 days.
  5. When the balloon deflates, fermentation is complete.
  6. Drink immediately (flat) or bottle and age for natural carbonation (2+ weeks).
  7. For smoother cider, refrigerate after fermentation for 48h to cold crash before bottling.

⚠️ TIPS & SAFETY

  • Avoid juice with preservatives (Asda's juice is safe).
  • If balloons swell too much, poke an extra hole or loosen briefly.
  • Store in a dark area at stable temperatures.
  • Label your bottles with brew date + notes.

🌸 FLAVOUR BOOSTING ADDITIONS (Kopparberg Style)

  • Before bottling, try:
    • Frozen berries (raspberry, strawberry, blackcurrant)
    • Cider flavour drops or essence (optional, ~£2–3 per bottle, lasts dozens of batches)
    • Stevia or erythritol for sweetness that doesn’t ferment (~£1.50+)
    • 1 tsp glycerin for smoothness (~£0.05/batch)
  • Serve cold, optionally over ice with mint
  • Age for 2–4 weeks to let flavours mellow and develop
